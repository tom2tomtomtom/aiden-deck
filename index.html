The phantom system

It believes it has lived a decades-long career.

AIDEN cultivates hundreds of phantom memories from an agency's own culture, documents, team interviews, and second brain. Each is a perspective with real expertise, an origin story, and a point of view.

When phantoms collide on a brief, it voices the disagreement instead of resolving it. The work has been argued over before it reaches you.

Product 01 / 09

Colleague.
A colleague, not a chatbot.

Trained on an agency's own culture through team interviews and internal documents, Colleague develops genuine creative conviction. It pushes back on weak briefs and defends bold ideas, in your agency's voice rather than a generic one.

Each client lives in a persistent workspace, so it already knows the brand when you open it. It evolves with feedback, and thinks overnight about the day's unresolved tensions.

Two modes: a Collaborator who builds with you, a Challenger who takes you on.

Product 02 / 09

Chat.
The thinking partner in the room.

Not ChatGPT with a skin. AIDEN's strategy-first worldview is built in, and hundreds of phantoms fire on every message to bring conviction and real pushback, not compliance.

It remembers the agency's tastes, defends creative choices, and works by voice or text.

A blank-slate chatbot agrees with you. This one has an opinion.

Product 03 / 09

Pitch.
Brief to pitch, one workflow.

A guided seven-step run takes a brief through strategy, territories, big idea, copy, and overview to a presentation-ready deck. Strategic decisions carry forward at every step, so nothing gets lost on the way to the room.

Run it collaboratively with approval at each stage, or let it generate end to end. Exports straight to PowerPoint.

The whole campaign, from blank brief to finished deck.

Product 04 / 09

Brief Sharpener.
A sharp brief is half the work.

It turns a vague brief into a single-minded one before the team starts. AIDEN finds the human tension, sharpens the proposition, and surfaces the questions a weak brief is hiding.

Better input at the start. Better everything downstream.

Product 05 / 09

Synthetic Research.
Test it before the meeting.

Eight distinct AI personas react to a concept, each with different demographics, values, and scepticism. You get a pressure score, the strengths, the vulnerabilities, and the exact questions the client will ask.

A strategist ran a territory through Synthetic Research at 9pm. By 10am they had killed the weakest route, strengthened the strongest, and walked in with data. The ECD asked where the research came from.

Product 06 / 09

Enter the brand names. AIDEN scrapes their live ads across Meta, TikTok, Google, and LinkedIn, analyses positioning, extracts palettes, and maps the landscape.

  • What is the uncharted territory?
  • What is the boldest move?
  • What tension can the client own that nobody else has?
